ELIGIBILITY

Generally, the Little Bird Foundation considers applications from anyone who is in need.

However, each month, our target group will change. For example, this month's target group is widows.















OUR OBJECTIVES

1


To provide care, shelter and sustenance for underprivileged persons through the establishment and management of assisted living centres and the provision of sustainable assistance programmes.


2


To promote, motivate, provide and meet needs of the under privileged especially children and the elderly, their civil rights and educational development.


3


To empower and motivate the less privilege in the society towards a reliance and sustainable lifestyle through well organise grassroots programs such as free education and skills acquisition training.


4


To improve the capacity of health facilities and to promote and provide medical support services to the less privileged through the initiation of free healthcare and medical enlightening programs.


5


To work with registered and authorized laboratory centres and hospital, including psychiatric hospitals for proper medical attention for same classes of persons.


6


To promote and support humanitarian programmes within the society in Nigeria through advocacy and citizen bureau.


7


To promote and improve the availability of all basic amenities such as food, water, health, education, good housing and motorable roads and all sure other amenities are made available to the under privileged and grassroots of the communities.


















THE PERSON BEHIND IT ALL

This is the person behind it all: Engr. Azuka Obasuyi! She knows firsthand about the difficulties that immigrant students face when they enter a new country, and decided to do something not only for other immigrant students, but for anyone in need.

Her nickname growing up was 'little bird', and she felt that like the birds who supported her when she was a little bird learning to stretch her wings, she wanted to do the same for other little birds learning how to fly now.

Now, the Little Bird Foundation is spreading its wings, hoping to reach other little birds in need of support, assistance, and guidance in our own way, and following her vision.
